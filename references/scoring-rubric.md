# ADA Scoring Rubric

## Per-criterion scale (0–3)

Score each of the 6 criteria against `reviewer-checklist.md`:

| Score | Meaning |
|:---:|---|
| **0** | An anti-pattern is present (KB Section 4), or the criterion is ignored entirely. |
| **1** | Baseline — native and functional, but unremarkable in this dimension. |
| **2** | Strong, intentional craft. A judge would notice. |
| **3** | Signature, best-in-class — *the reason* a judge remembers the app. |

## The "category bet"

Winners are not elite in all six categories. They pick **one category to be extraordinary in** (the
category bet) and are at least competent everywhere else. So the target shape is:

- **2–3 on the category bet** (the dimension the app is built to win on), and
- **≥1 on all other five, with no 0s.**

A profile of straight 1s ("competent, native, inoffensive") is a *non-contender* — pleasant but
forgettable. A profile with a 3 and a couple of 0s is also out — the 0s (anti-patterns) gate it. The
contender shape is one peak plus a clean floor.

## Combining into a verdict

1. **Contention floor:** every criterion ≥1 (no anti-patterns anywhere). If any criterion is 0, the
   headline finding is "clear the gate first" — fixing the 0 outranks polishing a peak.
2. **Peak:** at least one criterion at 2–3, ideally aligned with the chosen category bet.
3. **Overall /18** is a rough temperature, not a threshold — a 14/18 of all-2s is *less* award-credible
   than a 12/18 that is one 3 + five clean 1s+. Always read the *shape*, not just the sum.

## How to coach from the score

Order the action plan as:
1. **Clear the gates** — eliminate every 0 / anti-pattern and every cross-cutting-gate failure first.
   These are disqualifiers; nothing else matters until they're gone.
2. **Make one thing extraordinary** — concentrate remaining effort on the category bet to push it from
   2 → 3 (the signature dimension a judge remembers).
3. **Round up the floor** — lift any remaining 1s toward 2 where cheap, especially Inclusivity and
   Interaction (the most universally weighted).

End every review with the honest question: **"Would a judge remember this app — and for what?"** If you
can't name the one memorable thing, the category bet isn't sharp enough yet.
